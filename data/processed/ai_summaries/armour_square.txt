# Armour Square

With 526 active business licenses concentrated heavily in food and beverage, health and personal care, and miscellaneous services, Armour Square functions as a modest commercial hub with limited economic diversity (Shannon entropy: 1.19). Residents engage most with nearby amenities like Millennium Park, the waterfront, and neighboring neighborhoods like Wicker Park and Logan Square, suggesting the area serves as a residential base for accessing broader Chicago attractions. Visitors, by contrast, focus almost exclusively on Chinatown's dining scene—dim sum, Chinese restaurants, and the neighborhood's cultural district—indicating that tourists view Armour Square primarily as a gateway to Chinese food and cuisine rather than as a destination neighborhood itself. This divergence suggests residents value the neighborhood's connectivity to citywide attractions while visitors are drawn specifically to its ethnic commercial character and food identity.
