# Douglas Neighborhood Profile

With 286 active business licenses and a low business diversity index of 1.03, Douglas relies heavily on a concentrated set of sectors—particularly Food & Beverage, Automotive & Transportation, and Other services—suggesting limited economic variety. Residents engage most with discussions around public transportation access, community identity, and neighborhood dynamics, while the 121 active Airbnb listings (median $120/night) reveal that visitors are notably drawn to the neighborhood's proximity to major attractions like McCormick Place and the Green Line, as well as its cultural amenities like African restaurants and the Bronzeville area—cultural and transit assets that don't feature prominently in resident conversations. The neighborhood maintains overall positive community sentiment, with discussions anchored in local institutions and transportation infrastructure rather than the visitor-focused lens on dining and convention center access.
