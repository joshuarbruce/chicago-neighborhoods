# Greater Grand Crossing

With 591 active business licenses across a modest mix of sectors—led by food and beverage, automotive, and miscellaneous services—the neighborhood maintains a small commercial foundation that reflects limited economic diversity. Residents' online discussions center heavily on policing practices, consent decrees, and interactions with city agencies, painting a picture of a community engaged with accountability and governance issues. The 53 active Airbnb listings at a median nightly rate of $99 suggest a modest visitor market, where guests emphasize security features like gated properties and alarm systems—concerns that differ noticeably from the policing and civic accountability issues dominating resident conversations. Overall community sentiment registers as positive despite these underlying tensions around public safety and institutional oversight.
