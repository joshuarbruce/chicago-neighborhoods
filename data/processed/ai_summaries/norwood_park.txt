# Norwood Park

With 587 active business licenses and a moderate diversity index, the neighborhood supports a solid local economy anchored by food and beverage, automotive, and miscellaneous services. Residents engage most frequently with the park district and local recreational spaces like Foundry Park, suggesting these amenities play a meaningful role in community life. Notably, Airbnb visitors focus heavily on proximity to testing centers and medical examination sites—a pattern largely absent from resident conversations—indicating the neighborhood draws transient students and professionals for specific services rather than leisure tourism. The median nightly rate of $94 and modest 57 active listings reflect modest short-term rental activity compared to other Chicago neighborhoods.
