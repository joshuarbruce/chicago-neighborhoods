# West Pullman

With 311 active business licenses across a moderately diverse economy anchored by food and beverage, automotive, and other services, West Pullman supports local commerce at a modest scale. Residents engage deeply with the neighborhood's cultural and recreational institutions—particularly the Days Art Institute, holiday train events, and community spaces like Noble Square and the Central Manufacturing District—suggesting strong attachment to local gathering places and seasonal traditions. The neighborhood's limited short-term rental market (7 active Airbnb listings at a $140 median nightly rate) reflects minimal tourism infrastructure compared to visitor-focused amenities; guest reviews focus heavily on functional accommodations and appliance availability, whereas residents rarely mention such details, instead discussing community events, cultural programming, and neighborhood landmarks.
