# West Town, Chicago

With 3,119 active business licenses and a business diversity index of 1.23, West Town supports a robust commercial landscape anchored by food and beverage establishments alongside health and personal care services. Residents highlight the neighborhood's Polish heritage and dining destinations like Club Lucky and Big Star, while also expressing concerns about the rental market—a tension reflected in the 851 active Airbnb listings commanding a median nightly price of $171. Visitors emphasize walkability and the concentration of coffee shops and bars, aspects that feature less prominently in resident conversations, suggesting that tourists are drawn to the neighborhood's emerging urban amenities while longtime community members remain more focused on affordability and cultural anchors. Overall community sentiment registers as positive despite these underlying pressures.
