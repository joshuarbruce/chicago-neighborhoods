Chicago's 77 Neighborhoods

Business activity and community sentiment — a data portrait

About the Project

This project profiles every one of Chicago's 77 official community areas using two public data sources: active business license records from the Chicago Data Portal and community discussion pulled from Reddit. Each neighborhood slide shows an AI-generated summary, a business category breakdown, and the most distinctive phrases from Reddit discussion — all computed from data, not general knowledge.

The goal is to let the data speak for each neighborhood: which business types dominate, how the community talks about itself, and where the Reddit signal is strong or thin.

How It Works

Stage 1
Business License Collection

~85,000 active licenses pulled from the Chicago Data Portal Socrata API and filtered to license_status = AAC.

Stage 2
Business Classification

~200 license types collapsed into 10 categories via keyword matching. Shannon entropy used as a business diversity index.

Stage 3
Reddit Collection

Up to 1,000 posts per neighborhood from r/chicago searches and dedicated neighborhood subreddits. Paginated public JSON API, no OAuth.

Stage 4
Sentiment Analysis

Posts tokenized with tidytext. AFINN scores (−5 to +5) normalized by word count to prevent length bias. Bing lexicon used as a secondary signal.

Stage 5
Distinctive Phrases (TF-IDF)

Bi- and tri-gram phrases scored by TF-IDF across all 77 neighborhoods. Overlapping sub-phrases deduplicated so results favor longer, more specific phrases.

Stage 6
AI Summaries

Claude Haiku generates a 3–4 sentence summary per neighborhood from structured data only. Prompt explicitly prohibits general knowledge. Summaries are cached.

Data Quality

Reddit activity varies dramatically by neighborhood. Well-known areas like Logan Square and Hyde Park have hundreds of posts; some South and West Side neighborhoods have very few. Each neighborhood is flagged:

  • full — business data + Reddit data (≥10 posts)
  • low_reddit — fewer than 10 Reddit posts; sentiment used cautiously
  • business_only — no usable Reddit data; AI summary omits community signals
